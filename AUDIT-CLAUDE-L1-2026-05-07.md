**[HIGH]** `dolphin` — "a smart sea mammal that swims and breathes air" → Same "mammal" issue. → Fix: "a smart animal that lives in the sea and breathes air"

## 3. Example Sentence Issues

**[MEDIUM]** `wake up` — Example uses "woke up" (irregular past tense) instead of "wake up". A Grade 2 ESL reader looking for the exact words "wake up" won't find them. → Fix: "I have to wake up early to catch the school bus."

**[MEDIUM]** `run out` — Example uses "ran out" (irregular past). Same issue. → Fix: "We might run out of milk if nobody goes to the store."

**[MEDIUM]** `cookie` — Example uses "cookies" (plural) not "cookie". The target word is singular. → Fix: "Grandma baked a chocolate chip cookie just for me."

**[LOW]** `hear` — Example is unusually long and specific (basketball game). → Fix: "I can hear the birds singing outside my window."

**[LOW]** `lose` — Example is wordy and negative construction is complex for Grade 2. → Fix: "I don't want to lose my favorite pen."

**[LOW]** `teach` — "teach me how to write my first code" — "code" in programming context is age-appropriate but niche. → Consider: "My big sister will teach me how to ride a bike."

**[MEDIUM]** `borrow` — imageKeyword "handing a book to friend" → This looks identical to giving/lending, not borrowing. → Fix: "friend handing book saying borrow"

**[MEDIUM]** `lend` — imageKeyword "handing pencil to classmate" → Visually identical to "borrow" and "share" and "give." → Fix: "child offering pencil to friend" (both images need to be visually distinct)

**[LOW]** `sink` — imageKeyword "toy boat sinking in water" → Could be confused with kitchen sink. Current keyword is actually fine since it specifies "sinking." No fix needed.

**[LOW]** `glance` — imageKeyword "quick look" → Very generic; Google results may not clearly convey "glance." → Fix: "person glancing sideways"

**[LOW]** `dry` — imageKeyword "dry cracked ground" → Shows drought, not the everyday concept of "dry." → Fix: "dry towel on clothesline"
