## 🟠 SERIOUS: Vague/Inaccurate Definitions (7)

| Word | Current Definition | Problem |
|------|-------------------|---------|
| `gorgeous` | "very nice" | Too vague. Means "extremely beautiful." Mark can't distinguish this from "pleasant." |
| `magnificent` | "very nice and impressive" | Still vague. Should be "extremely impressive and grand." |
| `controversy` | "a big fight among people" | Not a fight. It's a strong disagreement or public debate. |
| `essential` | "something you fully need" | "Fully" is awkward. → "something you must have" |
| `creature` | "any living animal" | Too narrow. Creatures include imaginary beings (dragons, monsters). |
| `diverse` | "having many unlike kinds" | "Unlike" is unnatural for ESL. → "many different kinds" |
| `liberal` | "open to new ideas and generous" | Definition = political/philosophical sense. Example uses "liberal amount" = generous quantity. **Sense mismatch.** Pick one meaning. |

---

## 🟠 SERIOUS: Age/Level Inappropriateness

### adapt — AI example
> "A good AI system can adapt to new situations it has never seen before."

A 10yo ESL boy doesn't need AI jargon. Use a relatable context (animal, kid moving to new school).

### ~71 words are academic/SAT-level — too hard for MAP 197

These belong in Level 3–4, not Level 2. A 10yo ESL at MAP 197 (≈ 4th–5th grade reading) will not encounter these in context:

`empirical, ideology, hierarchy, levy, liberal, manifest, inherent, inhibit, invoke, induce, integral, consolidate, contemplate, contradict, convene, comply, compensate, coincide, conceive, consequent, constraint, consult, correspond, criteria, currency, deficiency, denote, derive, deviate, devote, diminish, dispose, distort, diverse, domain, dominate, duration, entity, equate, erode, explicit, facilitate, finite, fluctuate, framework, furthermore, hence, hypothesis, implication, impose, incentive, incorporate, initiate, innovation, intervene, isolate, magnitude, ambiguous, amend, approximate, aspire, assert, authorize, beneficial, bias, capacity, clarify, commentary, compile, complement, controversy`

**That's 71/400 words (18%) that are misleveled.** Mark will hit a wall of frustration. These are AWL (Academic Word List) / Tier 3 vocabulary — college-prep material, not 5th-grade ESL.

## 🟡 MINOR: Near-Duplicate Definitions

| Pair | Definitions |
|------|------------|
| `normally` / `typically` / `usually` | "the way things most times happen" / "the way something most times happens" / "most of the time" — three words, same meaning, same level |
| `constantly` / `continuously` | "all the time without stopping" / "going on and on without a break" — nearly identical |
| `presently` / `currently` | "at this moment or very soon" / "happening right now" — same |
| `briefly` / `shortly` | Both = "for a short time" / "very soon" — overlapping |
| `consent` / `permission` | "permission to do something" / "being allowed to do something" — consent's definition literally uses "permission" |

Mark is grinding 400 words. Near-duplicates waste his time and add no discriminative value.
