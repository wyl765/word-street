## Pattern Analysis

The vast majority of INACCURATE definitions stem from overly aggressive simplified-English processing that replaced normal words with awkward circumlocutions:
- "quality" → "how good something is"
- "physical" → "about the body"
- "energy" → "life and power"
- "speech" → "talking in front of people"
- "decision" → "what you pick" / "things you pick after thinking"
- "honest/honesty" → "telling what is true"
- "tendency" → "what you usually do to"
- "visible" → "able to be seen"
- "resources" → "things you can use"
- "strict" → "having many rules"
- "conscious" → "awake and aware"
- "reputation" → "what people think about you"
- "relationship" → "tie between people"
- "belief" → "idea about what is right"
- "occasion" → "a special time"
- "atmosphere" → "the air around the earth"
- "genuine" → contradictions like "not fake" alongside "not real"

All 128 INACCURATE definitions have been fixed in place.
