# THE EXECUTIONER'S VERDICT

## Kill List (FAIL — must die)

| word | file | dimension | what's wrong | how to fix |
|------|------|-----------|--------------|------------|
| mantle | words-level2.js | Factually WRONG | Definition says "a shelf above the place where a fire burns in a house" — that's a **mantel** (or mantelpiece). A "mantle" is a cloak or the Earth's layer between crust and core. Wrong word entirely. | Change word to "mantel" OR change definition to "a cloak worn over the shoulders" |
| corona | words-level3a.js | Example factually WRONG | Example: "a bright corona glowed around the dark circle of the moon" — During a solar eclipse the corona is visible around the **SUN** (which is blocked by the moon). The moon is what blocks, not what the corona surrounds. | Fix to: "…a bright corona glowed around the sun as the moon blocked its center." |
| dolphin | words-level1.js | Definition factually WRONG | "a gray sea animal with a **rounded snout**" — Dolphins have elongated, pointed snouts (rostrums). Porpoises have rounded snouts. This teaches the wrong distinguishing feature. | Change "rounded snout" to "long pointed nose" or "long snout" |
| amber | words-level2.js | Definition/example MISMATCH | Definition: "hard, golden-brown material formed from ancient tree sap" (= fossilized resin, the noun). Example: "The leaves turned a beautiful amber in the fall" (= the color adjective). These are different senses. A child reading this will be confused. | Either change definition to "a warm golden-brown color; also a hard material from ancient tree sap" OR change example to use the material sense |
| chord | words-level2.js | Factually WRONG | "two or more musical notes played together" — Two notes is an **interval**, not a chord. A chord requires **three or more** notes. Every music textbook agrees. | Change to "three or more musical notes played together" |
| taste | words-level2.js | Definition WRONG | "what food feels like in your mouth" — That describes TEXTURE/mouthfeel. Taste is the flavor sensation (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, savory) detected by taste buds. | Change to "the flavor you notice when food touches your tongue" |
| biscuit | words-level2.js | Definition WRONG | "a small, soft bread roll" — A biscuit is NOT a roll. In American English it's a type of quick bread (leavened with baking powder, flaky). In British English it's a crisp cookie. Neither is a "roll." | Change to "a small, soft, flaky bread made without yeast" |
| vitamin | words-level2b.js | Def uses harder word than target + redundant grammar | "a **nutrient** in food that your body needs in small amounts to stay healthy **that keeps you strong and well**" — (1) "nutrient" is harder than "vitamin" for L2 kids. (2) The second clause "that keeps you strong and well" dangles awkwardly after "to stay healthy" (redundant). | Change to "something in food that your body needs in small amounts to stay healthy" |
