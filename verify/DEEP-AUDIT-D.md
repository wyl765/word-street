## Auditor Notes

The most common issue pattern is **garble residue** — definitions that were clearly simplified from more precise language but lost grammatical coherence in the process. The "avoid technical terms" approach sometimes produced phrasings like "a small part of something" (for fraction), "yellow dust on flowers" (for pollen), and "a sloping top" (for roof) that are more confusing than the original terms.

**Recommendation:** Fix the 54 WARN items before publication. Priority fixes:
1. Garbled definitions (historic, governor, numerator, denominator, distributive) — these are actively confusing
2. Grammar errors (let the cat out of the bag, effervescent, flotsam, fluent) — these read as broken English
3. The expel example (anatomical error) — factually wrong detail
