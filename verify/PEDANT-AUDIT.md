## False Claims (definition states something not always true)

| word | file | claim | counterexample |
|------|------|-------|----------------|
| bunny | words-level1.js | "a small rabbit" | Bunny is informal for ANY rabbit regardless of size. Large rabbits are also called bunnies |
| robin | words-level1.js | "a small bird with a red or orange chest" | American robins are MEDIUM-sized (25cm). "Small" is incorrect for the most common referent |
| worm | words-level1.js | "a tiny soft animal with no legs that lives in dirt" | Giant Gippsland earthworm reaches 3 meters. "Tiny" is demonstrably false for the category |
| snail | words-level1.js | "a tiny animal with a shell that moves slowly" | Giant African snail is 20cm+. "Tiny" is false for the general category |
| spider | words-level1.js | "a small animal with eight legs that makes webs" | 1) Goliath bird-eater is hand-sized (not small). 2) Many spiders don't make webs (jumping spiders, wolf spiders) |
| caterpillar | words-level1.js | "a small animal with many legs" | Caterpillars have exactly 6 true legs + up to 10 prolegs. "Many legs" confuses with centipedes/millipedes |
| ladybug | words-level1.js | "a small red bug with black spots" | Ladybugs come in orange, yellow, and even black. "Red" is not universal |
| celery | words-level1.js | "a long green crunchy vegetable" | Store-bought celery is often pale/white/yellow, not green |
| pepper | words-level1.js | "a crunchy vegetable that... is hollow inside" | Bell peppers contain seeds, membrane, and ribs inside—not truly hollow |
| moose | words-level1.js | "a very big deer with large flat antlers" | Female moose do NOT have antlers. Claim only true for males |
| mushroom | words-level1.js | "a type of fungus... that grows in damp places" | Desert truffles and other fungi grow in arid conditions. Not all need dampness |
| cottage | words-level1.js | "a small house, often far from the city" | Many cottage-style homes are in cities. "Far from city" is a romantic myth |
| meadow | words-level1.js | "a big open field of grass and flowers" | Meadows can be small. Size is not a defining feature |
| desert | words-level1.js | implies hot via "hot desert except cactus" in example | Antarctica is a desert. Arctic deserts exist. Not all deserts are hot |
| enormous | words-level1.js | "so big it is hard to believe" | Enormous means very large—doesn't require disbelief. That's "incredible" |
| gigantic | words-level1.js | "extremely big, bigger than big" | "Bigger than big" is circular/meaningless. Provides no actual reference point |
| adequate | words-level2a.js | "just barely enough to get the job done" | Adequate means sufficient/satisfactory. "Barely" adds a negative connotation not in the word |
| levy | words-level2a.js | "to collect money by order of the government" | Levy means to IMPOSE a tax, not collect it. Collection happens after imposition |
| domestic | words-level2a.js | "raised by people and living in homes, not wild" | Domestic also means relating to one's home or country. Only animal sense given as if complete |
| average | words-level1.js | "not too much, not too little, in the middle" | Average is a mathematical mean (sum/count). Definition describes "moderate" or "medium" |

## Example Failures (example doesn't prove the definition)

| word | file | issue |
|------|------|-------|
| boiling | words-level1.js | Def says "so hot that bubbles form and steam rises" but example says "too hot to touch"—water at 60°C is too hot to touch but not boiling |
| hollow | words-level1.js | "Made a drum sound" doesn't prove hollowness—solid wood resonates too. Should show seeing/reaching into empty space |
| during | words-level1.js | Def says "while something else is going on" but example "fell asleep during the movie" shows one event within a timeframe, not two simultaneous activities |
| throughout | words-level1.js | "Flowers grew throughout the whole garden"—"throughout the whole" is tautological. Throughout already means "in every part" |
| among | words-level1.js | "Found his toy among all the others" demonstrates finding/searching, not being in the middle of. Better: "The red ball sat among the blue ones" |
| average | words-level1.js | Example uses "average day" meaning ordinary/unremarkable—contradicts definition about "in the middle" of amounts |
| diminish | words-level2a.js | Example: "did not diminish—it actually went up." Uses NEGATION of the word. Demonstrates what it does NOT mean |
| ideology | words-level2a.js | "Practice hard, play fair, never give up" is a team MOTTO, not an ideology. Ideologies are political/social belief systems |
| legend | words-level1.js | Example about a dragon is a myth/fairy tale, not a legend. Legends feature ostensibly historical figures/places |
| numerous | words-level2a.js | "Too many to count" describes "countless/innumerable," not "numerous" which implies a large but countable number |
| generate | words-level2a.js | Using AI as the example is oddly meta for a vocabulary learning app aimed at children |
| scarce | words-level2a.js | Trading card example demonstrates rarity driving price up—this is "rare/valuable," not specifically "scarce" (insufficient supply vs demand) |
| positive | words-level2a.js | Definition conflates "sure/certain" and "hopeful" (two different meanings). Example only shows "certain" sense |
| bark | words-level1.js | Defined as "the short loud sound" (noun) but is primarily a verb. Example uses noun form fine, but definition frames it oddly |

## Unusual/Archaic Sense Issues

| word | file | issue |
|------|------|-------|
| lace | words-level1.js | Primary sense of "lace" is decorative openwork fabric. "Shoelace" is a compound word—defining "lace" as a shoe string is the secondary sense |
| stable | words-level1.js | As adjective "stable" (not changing, firm, steady) is FAR more common in everyday usage than the noun (horse building). Noun is valid but not primary |
| bark | words-level1.js | Equally common senses: dog sound AND tree covering. Defining only one leaves students confused when they encounter the other |
| scale | words-level1.js | For most children, "scale" means a weighing device or musical scale. Fish/snake scale is valid but arguably not the primary sense encountered |
| content | words-level1.js | Adjective "content" (satisfied) vs noun "content" (what's inside something). Both equally common. Only one sense taught |
| draft | words-level2a.js | Also means: current of air, military conscription, beer from a tap. "First version of writing" is ONE sense among many equally common ones |
