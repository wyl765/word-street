# GPT Audit — Level 2 Bank (words-level2.js)
Date: 2026-05-07
Scope: ONLY problems found (no praise).

## 1) Definition ↔ example meaning mismatch (or definition quality issues)

- **act**
  - Definition: “to do something”
  - Example: “I will act like a lion in the play.”
  - Problem: Example uses **act = perform/pretend**, not “do something.”

- **action**
  - Definition: “something that is done”
  - Example: “The action in the story starts when the door opens.”
  - Problem: Example uses **action = events/excitement in a story**, not a general “something that is done.”

- **crash**
  - Definition: “to hit with a loud noise” (verb)
  - Example: “The wave made a crash on the rocks.” (noun)
  - Problem: Part-of-speech mismatch; definition does not match example usage.

- **course**
  - Definition: “a path or a class”
  - Example: “The obstacle course had cones to run around.”
  - Problem: “obstacle course” is neither a simple “path” nor a “class”; definition is too broad and misses this common meaning.

- **block**
  - Definition: “a solid piece or a city section”
  - Example: “I used a block of wood to prop the door open.”
  - Problem: Multiple unrelated meanings bundled into one entry; example only supports one meaning.

- **deal**
  - Definition: “an agreement or a lot”
  - Example: “We made a deal: I clean up, then I play.”
  - Problem: Second meaning (“a lot”) is vague and not supported by example; likely confuses ESL learners.

- **paragraph**
  - Definition: “a set of lines about one topic”
  - Problem: Inaccurate/unstable (line breaks change). A paragraph is a **group of sentences** about one topic.

- **sentence**
  - Definition: “words that make a complete thought”
  - Problem: Grammar/clarity: should be “**a group of words that makes** a complete thought.”

- **citizen**
  - Definition: “a person who belongs to a place”
  - Example: “As a citizen, my mom votes in our town.”
  - Problem: Too vague and potentially misleading. “Citizen” is normally membership in a **country/nation** (sometimes city in specific contexts). Example implies “citizen of our town.”

- **balloon**
  - Definition: “a rubber ball filled with air”
  - Problem: Inaccurate. A balloon is not necessarily a “ball” and not always rubber; can be different shapes and may be filled with helium.

- **careful**
  - Definition: “paying close focus”
  - Problem: Unnatural/incorrect collocation (“close focus”). Should be “paying close **attention**” / “being careful.”

- **also**
  - Definition: “in addition,; as well”
  - Problem: Punctuation/typo (“in addition,;”).

- **data**
  - Example: “We wrote data about the weather each day.”
  - Problem: Unnatural phrasing; typical collocation is “**recorded/collected** data.”

- **width**
  - Example: “The width of the door is wider than my arms.”
  - Problem: Awkward/tautological (“width … wider”).

- **force**
  - Example: “A force makes the swing move forward.”
  - Problem: Does not clearly demonstrate “force = a push or pull.” Reads like a vague science statement.

- **triple**
  - Definition: “three times as much”
  - Example: “The recipe needs to triple the sugar for a big batch.”
  - Problems:
    - Example uses **triple as a verb** (“to triple”), but definition is phrased like an adjective.
    - Sentence is awkward (“needs to triple the sugar”).

- **biscuit**
  - Definition: “a small, soft bread roll”
  - Problem: Dialect ambiguity: in British English, “biscuit” = cookie. For ESL, this needs disambiguation or a different headword.

- **bobsled**
  - Example: “The team raced the bobsled down the hill.”
  - Problem: Misleading; bobsleds race on an **icy track**, not simply “down the hill.”
