## Summary

- **Critical issues:** 35 (mostly Level 2 words that belong in Level 3+ due to academic/formal/specialized register)
- **Major issues:** 34 (inappropriate examples using AI/financial/sports jargon; some additional level misplacements)
- **Minor issues:** 22 (typos, verbose imageKeywords, near-synonym clustering, definition errors)

### Pattern: AI/Tech Examples
A systematic problem exists where ~15 examples reference AI, programming, neural networks, or financial markets. These are clearly written for an adult audience and are not relatable for a 10-year-old ESL student. All should be replaced with age-appropriate contexts.

### Pattern: Level Inflation in 2A
Level 2A contains approximately 30–35 words from academic word lists (AWL) that are typically taught to university-level ESL students. These include: empirical, hierarchy, ideology, consolidate, inherent, integral, fluctuate, facilitate, etc. These should be moved to Level 3 or 4.

### No "key" → "important" Bug Pattern Found
I did not find instances of the word "key" being defined as "important" in these files.

### No Inappropriate Content for Children Found
Beyond the "conceive" dual-meaning risk and "explicit" connotation noted above, no sexually inappropriate or violent content was detected.
