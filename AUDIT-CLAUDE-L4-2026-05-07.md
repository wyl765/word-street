## 4. FORMATTING & STRUCTURAL ISSUES

### Inconsistencies
- **communiqué vs communique**: The word entry says "communique" but the example uses "communiqué" with proper accent. Pick one consistently.
- **per capita**: Two-word entry in 4c — is this intentional? Most vocabulary lists treat multi-word terms differently.
- **Draconian**: Capitalized in example ("Draconian punishment") but lowercase in word entry ("draconian"). Should be consistent — lowercase is standard as an adjective.

- **dormant** (4a) and **dormancy** (4a) — both in the same file; redundant

**Overall verdict:** This list is fundamentally mislabeled. The majority of entries are college-prep or graduate-level vocabulary unsuitable for 10-year-olds. The simpler entries (abandon, career, cargo, etc.) are genuinely Level 4, but they are swamped by GRE words like "pulchritude," "contumacious," "palimpsest," and "consanguinity." The list needs to be split: keep ~300 age-appropriate words as Level 4, and move the rest to Levels 6–8+.

Additionally, there are dozens of grammar errors, sentence fragments, duplicate words in definitions, and garbled phrasing that would not pass copy-editing at any reputable publisher.
