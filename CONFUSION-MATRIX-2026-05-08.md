## Analysis: Definitions That Need Improvement

### 🔴 Must Fix (caused error)

**Q9 — "nervous": "worried and nervous"**  
- **Problem:** The definition is **circular** — it uses the target word in its own definition. A kid reading "worried and nervous" will think "this can't be 'nervous' because that would be defining itself." They'll pick "anxious" instead.
- **Fix suggestion:** "feeling butterflies in your stomach before something happens" or "feeling a little scared about what might happen next"

### 🟡 High Confusion Risk (got it right but hesitated)

**Q3 — "turtle": "an animal with a hard shell"**  
- **Problem:** Snail also has a shell. Beetle's definition literally says "an insect with a hard shell." Only "animal" (vs "insect"/"tiny animal") distinguishes them, which is subtle for a 10-year-old.
- **Fix suggestion:** "a slow animal that can hide its head and legs inside its hard shell"

**Q7 — "enormous" vs "gigantic" vs "huge"**  
- **Problem:** Three of four options are near-synonyms for "very big." Kids rely on luck or memorization rather than understanding.
- **Fix suggestion:** Make definitions more distinct: enormous = "so big you can hardly believe it" (ok), gigantic = "as big as a giant" (leverage the root word), huge = "much much bigger than normal"
- **Alternative:** Don't put these three in the same question group.

**Q8 — "shiny": "bright and reflecting light"**  
- **Problem:** "bright" is literally one of the options! A kid might think "bright = bright." The word "bright" appears both in the definition and as a distractor.
- **Fix suggestion:** "it catches your eye because light bounces off it, like a mirror or new coin"

**Q16 — "choice": "something you pick"**  
- **Problem:** "pick" is both in the definition and in the answer options. A 10-year-old might select "pick" because the definition literally says "pick."
- **Fix suggestion:** "when you have to decide between two or more things" or "the thing you decided to take"

**Q18 — "vanish" vs "disappear"**  
- **Problem:** Nearly identical meanings. "Like magic" in the definition saves it, but a kid who knows "disappear" better might choose that.
- **Fix suggestion:** Keep "like magic" — it works. Or: "to suddenly go away so completely that nothing is left"

**Q19 — "search": "to look carefully for something"**  
- **Problem:** "look" is one of the options and the definition starts with "to look." Kids might pick "look."
- **Fix suggestion:** "to check everywhere, even hard places, to find something that is lost"

## Key Takeaway

**Main design rule violated:** Never use the target word (or an option word) inside the definition. This caused the only error (Q9) and nearly caused errors in Q8, Q16, Q19.

**Recommended fixes (priority order):**
1. Q9 nervous — circular definition → rewrite
2. Q16 choice — "pick" is both definition word and option → rewrite  
3. Q8 shiny — "bright" is both definition word and option → rewrite
4. Q19 search — "look" is both definition word and option → rewrite
5. Q3 turtle — "hard shell" overlaps with beetle/snail → add distinguishing detail
6. Q7 enormous — avoid grouping 3 synonyms together, or make definitions more distinct
