## 2) Grammar / typos / punctuation errors
- precious — Example: “The ring was precious to **his** ...” → wrong pronoun; should be **him**.
- material — Example: “... kept **his** very warm.” → wrong pronoun/object; should be “kept **him** very warm” (or rewrite).
- recognize — Example: “I didn't recognize **his** at first ...” → should be **him**.
- accumulate — Definition: “... more and more **overtime**” → should be **over time**.
- boundary — Example has incorrect comma: “between our yard**, and** the neighbor's yard.”
- require — Example has unnecessary comma: “requires three eggs**, and** a cup of milk.”
- consequent — Example punctuation is broken/ungrammatical: “The heavy rain**, and** the consequent flooding closed the road.”
- permanently — Example phrasing is awkward/redundant: “permanently **stayed** ...” (better: “stayed ... permanently” or “was permanent even after washing”).

## 3) Definition accuracy / sense mismatches
- vapor — Definition says “tiny drops of water floating in the air like a gas.” Water vapor is **gas** (not droplets); droplets are **mist/fog/steam** (as commonly seen). Current definition is scientifically inaccurate.
- permanently — Definition “stays the same and never ends” is inaccurate/odd. “Permanently” means “for a long time/forever; not temporary,” not “stays the same.”
- liberal — Definition mixes unrelated senses: “open to new ideas and generous,” but example uses only “a liberal amount” (= large/generous). Combining senses will confuse ESL learners (and the political meaning is nearby).
- presently — Definition “at this moment **or very soon**” is ambiguous across dialects. For kids/ESL, pick one meaning and stick to it.
- controversy — Definition “a big fight among people” is misleading/too narrow. A controversy is a **public disagreement/debate** about something.

## 4) imageKeyword problems (unclear, mismatched, or not imageable)
- simultaneously — imageKeyword “twins blowing candles” does not match the example (pool jump). Also keyword should match the card’s scenario.
- usually — imageKeyword “morning routine alarm clock and breakfast” does not match the example (pizza on Friday nights).
- generate — imageKeyword “generate energy” does not match the example (AI generating a painting) and shifts meaning.
- scarce — imageKeyword “scarce desert” is not clearly connected to the example (trading cards) and is vague.
- raw — imageKeyword “raw vegetables” mismatches example (raw chicken).
- bury — imageKeyword “burying treasure” mismatches example (squirrel burying nuts).
- hence / likewise / furthermore — imageKeywords like “hence therefore”, “likewise same”, “furthermore also” are not image-searchable/illustratable in a reliable way.

## 5) Age/level appropriateness (MAP 197, 10-year-old ESL)
Large portion of the list is far beyond typical “Level 2” ESL expectations (abstract academic vocabulary, civics/economics/politics, advanced morphology). Even if the intent is “academic vocabulary,” many items are not appropriate without heavier scaffolding.
