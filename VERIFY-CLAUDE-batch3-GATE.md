### Key Themes

1. **Level 3c has too many level 4+ words** (~20 words): Many advanced scientific/geographic terms (stratosphere, archipelago, paleontology, geothermal, etc.) and literary words (limpid, lithe, lucid, imbue, hyperbole) are placed at level 3 but belong at level 4.

2. **Level 4 files have many extremely rare/obscure words** (~25 words): Words like pulchritude, contumacious, termagant, internecine, nugatory, palimpsest, consanguinity, bowdlerize, ineluctable, fulminate, gainsay, impecunious, panegyric, abjure, apotheosis, atavism, celerity are SAT/GRE-level vocabulary that a 10-year-old with MAP 197 (grade 2 reading) will never encounter. Consider removing or placing in a "challenge/bonus" tier.

3. **One broken definition** (CRITICAL): "exponentiation" has grammatically broken definition text.

4. **Two awkward definitions** (HIGH): "alleviation" and "declination" have unclear phrasing.

5. **Cultural sensitivity**: Generally well-handled. Only "termagant" carries historical gender bias worth addressing.
