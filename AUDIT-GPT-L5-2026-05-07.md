# GPT Vocabulary Audit — Level 5 (A–D)
Date: 2026-05-07
Target learner: ~10 years old
Scope: ONLY problems (definition accuracy, example quality, imageKeyword, age appropriateness, grammar)

---

## words-level5a.js

- **administer**
  - Example is internally inconsistent: “principal” vs “boss” (a principal is the boss in that context). Also “administer the school” is awkward phrasing for elementary usage.

- **capitalism**
  - Definition is oversimplified/inaccurate: capitalism is not merely “people own their own businesses”; it’s an economic system centered on **private ownership**, **markets/competition**, and **profit**.

- **comprise**
  - Definition includes the disputed/commonly-corrected sense “to include.” Many educational publishers avoid teaching “comprise = include” because it leads to usage errors. Prefer “to be made up of.”

- **medium**
  - Definition is inaccurate/vague (“a way of talking or expressing thoughts”). For the given example (“the internet … medium”), the meaning is “a means/channel of communication” (also can mean “material used by an artist”).

- **implicit**
  - Definition is ungrammatical: “not straight stated.” Should be “not **directly** stated / implied.”

- **indispensable**
  - Definition is incorrect/ungrammatical: “cannot do with no.” Should be “absolutely necessary; cannot do without.”

- **respective**
  - Definition is ungrammatical/incorrect: “belonging apart to each one mentioned.” Should be “belonging separately to each of the people or things mentioned.”

## words-level5b.js

- **subdivide**
  - Definition has a dangling pronoun (“split **it**”). Should be phrased as “to divide something into smaller parts.”

- **successive**
  - Definition is missing an article (“without **a** gap”).

- **cardinal**
  - **imageKeyword mismatch**: definition includes the bird meaning, but example uses “cardinal rule”; imageKeyword “gold number one trophy” does not match either “cardinal rule” or the red bird.

- **circumvent**
  - Example encourages wrongdoing: “circumvent the blocked website at school” (promotes bypassing school safety controls). Replace with a non-rule-breaking context.

- **martial**
  - imageKeyword mismatch: “military training” points to war/soldiers, but the example is about **martial arts**.

- **prognosis**
  - Definition is wrong: not “a prediction about how something will grow.” It is a prediction about the likely course/outcome of an illness (or more broadly, how a situation will turn out).

- **renegade**
  - Definition is too narrow and loaded (“deserts and betrays a group”). Common meaning is broader: a person who leaves an organization/group or rejects rules/ideas; “betrays” is not always part of the meaning.
  - Example context (soldier switching sides) is more war-centered than needed for this age.

- **relentless**
  - imageKeyword mismatch: example is sports defense; imageKeyword “endless rain” points to weather.

- **unobtrusive**
  - Example uses informal abbreviation “cam.” For children/ESL materials, use “camera.”

## words-level5c.js

- **cascade**
  - Definition is ungrammatical: “one after one more.” Should be “one after another.”

- **circa**
  - Definition is incorrect/garbled: “about or about.” Should be “approximately; around (used with dates).”

- **clarification**
  - Definition is incorrect: not “a reason that makes something easier to get.” It’s an explanation or extra information that makes something clearer.

- **communism**
  - Definition is misleading/overly simplified: “government owns all property and shares it equally” is not a reliable definition across contexts. Better: a political/economic system aiming for **collective/community ownership** and **equal distribution**, often contrasted with private ownership.

- **corporal**
  - imageKeyword mismatch: entry includes “army rank,” but example is **corporal punishment**; imageKeyword “military rank” does not match the example.
  - Example topic (corporal punishment) is sensitive and may require careful framing for 10-year-olds.

- **enlighten**
  - Definition is incorrect/awkward: “to give someone what you know or grasp.” Should be “to give someone understanding; to inform.”

- **envoy**
  - Definition is ungrammatical/incomplete: “a messenger or person who speaks for sent on a mission.”

- **exposition**
  - Definition is incorrect: “a clear reason” confuses it with *explanation*. Exposition is (1) information that explains/introduces a topic, or (2) the opening/background part of a story.

- **genealogy**
  - Definition is missing key words: “the study of family the past and ancestors.” Should be “the study of family history and ancestors.”

- **patriot**
  - Example is ungrammatical (“helped out to serve”) and war-centered for this age.

- **peasant**
  - Definition is incorrect: “medieval or growing societies” is wrong phrasing (should be “medieval or agricultural societies”).

- **pension**
  - Definition wording is off (“normal payments”) and unclear. Should be “regular payments after retirement.”

- **epitome**
  - Example needs punctuation for clarity: “She was the epitome of kindness, always helping others.”

- **gladiator**
  - Definition trivializes violence: “battled for fun.” Better: fought for entertainment in ancient Rome.

- **detonate**
  - Age appropriateness: bomb/explosive framing is intense for 10-year-olds; if kept, ensure neutral/safety framing.

- **encryption**
  - Definition is incorrect/garbled: “converting facts into a secret code for a shield.” Should be “turning information into code to protect it.”

- **heresy**
  - Example adds harsh historical punishment (“punished severely”), which may be unnecessarily intense for 10-year-olds.

- **insurrection**
  - Age appropriateness: “violent uprising,” “put down by government forces,” and imageKeyword “armed revolt” are intense.

- **internship**
  - Definition contains a typo/garble: “work feel.”

- **ingenuity**
  - Example grammar error: “helped **his** build” → should be “helped **him** build.”

- **marauder**
  - Example is violent (“attacked the village”) and may be too intense for the target age; consider a less graphic context.

## words-level5d.js

- **cognition**
  - imageKeyword is overly specific/illustration-like (“glowing brain with gears”) rather than a simple, search-friendly keyword.

- **impunity**
  - Example is missing punctuation between independent clauses: “The bully acted with impunity; no one reported his behavior.”

- **jurisprudence**
  - Example is awkward/repetitive (“school law school”).

- **redundant**
  - Example is incorrect: it treats the word **gift** itself as redundant. The classic redundancy is **“free gift”** (or similar), not “gift.”

- **resilience**
  - Definition is inaccurate/narrow: resilience is the ability to recover from difficulties, not specifically “heal quickly from problems.”

- **emissary**
  - Definition is ungrammatical/incomplete (“…as a person who speaks for”).
  - Example has pronoun mismatch (“queen … bring **his** message”).
