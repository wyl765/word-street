## ROUND 5: ANSWER DISAMBIGUATION

### Same-level definition pairs that are dangerously similar:

**Level 5 — 106 pairs with >70% word overlap in definitions:**
- authenticate vs validate ("prove something is real/genuine" vs "prove something is correct/acceptable")
- coerce vs obligate
- confiscate vs confiscation (word + nominalization pairs)
- culminate vs culmination
- benevolent vs benevolence

**⚠️ CRITICAL ISSUE:** Word-form pairs (verb + noun form) like coerce/coercion, culminate/culmination, confiscate/confiscation have nearly identical definitions. In a multiple-choice quiz, a learner could not distinguish them.

**Fix needed:** Either (a) remove one form from each pair, or (b) make definitions structurally distinct (e.g., "coerce" = "to force someone..." vs "coercion" = "the act of forcing..."; ensure the POS difference is explicit).

## ROUND 12: GAME MECHANICS COMPATIBILITY

### MODE 1 (Image → Word): Duplicate imageKeywords 🔴

**272 duplicate imageKeyword values** across the bank. Examples:
- "goose" → goose (L1) AND attack (L2)
- "scissors" → scissors (L1), careful (L2), cut off (L2)
- "popcorn" → popcorn (L1), handful (L2), smell (L2)

**⚠️ ISSUE:** If the game shows an image of "scissors" and asks "pick the word," three different words could be correct. The game engine MUST filter by current level, but if "scissors" and "careful" are both active, ambiguity remains.

**Recommendation:** Ensure imageKeywords are unique within each level. Cross-level duplicates are acceptable if the game only shows same-level options.

**Recommendation:** These are fine for definition/image modes but the spelling game should weight them lower or provide hints for MAP 197.

Words like "during," "although," "whether," "perhaps," "meanwhile" have context-appropriate examples, but their imageKeywords are weak ("clock showing time passing", "whether choice"). Image modes should skip these; context mode is their primary assessment path. ✓
